Nature's Wonders

As we learn more and more of the impact humans are having on the planet, the more important it becomes that scientists explore how to create a sustainable future not just for humans but for the whole planet.
In our ecology section of the museum, we unpack the dangers we currently face including global warming, extinction of species, and pollution. But we also focus on the positive ways we can all contribute to a brighter future.

Pollinator Garden

Step into a vibrant oasis buzzing with life at the Pollinator Garden exhibit. Explore a diverse array of native plants specially selected to attract pollinators such as bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds. As you wander among the blooms, observe these vital pollinators in action, learning about their crucial role in ecosystem health and food production. Interactive displays offer tips on creating pollinator-friendly habitats in your own backyard, empowering visitors to become stewards of biodiversity.

DATE: 28th apr - 17th may

Climate Change Simulation

Embark on a journey into the future with the Climate Change Simulation exhibit. Through immersive multimedia experiences and interactive displays, witness the impacts of climate change on ecosystems around the world. Explore how rising temperatures, shifting precipitation patterns, and habitat loss are affecting plants, animals, and landscapes. Discover solutions for mitigating and adapting to climate change, and learn how individual actions can make a difference in addressing this global challenge.

DATE: 5th may - 20th june

Photo by Patrick Perkins on Unsplash

Biodiversity Wall

Marvel at the stunning diversity of life on Earth at the Biodiversity Wall exhibit. Towering images and information panels showcase the incredible array of species that inhabit our planet, from microscopic organisms to majestic mammals. As you explore the wall, learn about the importance of biodiversity for ecosystem resilience and human well-being. Gain insight into conservation efforts aimed at protecting species and habitats, and discover how you can help safeguard biodiversity for future generations.

DATE: 20th may - 30th june
